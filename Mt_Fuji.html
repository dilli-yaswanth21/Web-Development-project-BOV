Mt.Fuji

Mount Fuji is the highest mountain in Japan, with a summit elevation of 3,776.24 m (12,389 ft 3 in). It is the second-highest volcano located on an island in Asia (after Mount Kerinci on the island of Sumatra), and seventh-highest peak of an island on Earth. Mount Fuji is an active stratovolcano that last erupted from 1707 to 1708. The mountain is located about 100 km (62 mi) southwest of Tokyo and is visible from there on clear days. Mount Fuji is a very distinctive feature of the geography of Japan. It stands 3,776.24 m (12,389 ft) tall and is located near the Pacific coast of central Honshu, just southwest of Tokyo. The mountain is a popular tourist destination and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Mount Fuji is a sacred mountain in Shinto and Buddhism. It has been a pilgrimage site for centuries and is still considered to be a holy place by many Japanese people. The mountain is also a popular subject of art and photography. The mountain is made up of three successive volcanoes. The oldest volcano, Komitake, is located at the base of Mount Fuji. The second volcano, Ko-Fuji, was formed around 100,000 years ago. The third volcano, Shin-Fuji, is the youngest and is the mountain we see today. Mount Fuji last erupted in 1707. The eruption was a major event in Japanese history and caused widespread damage. The eruption also created a new crater at the summit of the mountain. Mount Fuji is a popular hiking destination. The mountain is open to climbers from July to September. The climb to the summit is challenging but rewarding. The views from the summit are spectacular and offer stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
