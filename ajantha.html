Ajantha Caves

The Ajanta Caves are a magnificent complex of Buddhist rock-cut cave temples located in Maharashtra, India. They are renowned for their stunning ancient frescoes, sculptures, and architectural marvels. The caves are situated in the Sahyadri hills of the Western Ghats, around 100 kilometers (62 miles) from the city of Aurangabad. The Ajanta Caves were carved out of volcanic basalt rock during two distinct periods in Indian history. The first phase of construction took place around the 2nd century BCE during the Satavahana dynasty, while the second phase occurred during the Vakataka dynasty in the 5th and 6th centuries CE. The caves served as a place of religious worship, monastic retreat, and artistic expression..
